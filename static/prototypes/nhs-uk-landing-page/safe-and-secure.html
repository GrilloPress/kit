Safe and secure

Completely confidential

The NHS takes the responsibility for protecting your confidential information very seriously. It is essential that patient data is kept safe and secure at all times. There are strict laws and regulations to ensure your patient health information is kept confidential. This means it can only be accessed by health care professionals directly involved in your care. These laws include the Data Protection Act (1998) and the Human Rights Act (1998).

The NHS protects and shields your privacy by:

  • removing personally identifiable information
  • adopting an independent review process
  • putting legal contracts in place before data is shared
  • applying strict data sharing agreements

Technology is used to restrict access and encrypt data so information can only be read with a code.

National IT systems are kept up-to-date and relevant to protect against viruses and new cyber threats.

Anyone accessing data must have appropriate training and be approved by the organisation.

Audit trails are in place that record every instance that personally identifiable data is viewed or used.

Information Governance

Information Governance (IG) across the NHS ensures your data is looked after in accordance with good practice and the law. Staff members, suppliers and clients are all updated on a regular basis to make sure we all abide by the rules and regulations. This applies to the way your information is collected and handled across the NHS. Frequent data sharing audits and annual reviews will ensure your data is being look after appropriately and for the right purposes.

Requests for data can be rejected if the information will be used for anything other than promoting health and care services in England. If an organisation cannot prove it will look after data to IG standards, the request will also be rejected.

Specialist bodies

All data requests for identifiable information are reviewed carefully by specialist bodies:

Standardisation Committee for Care Information (SCCI)

SCCI is run by the National Information Board and it reviews requests for us to collect health information.

Independent Group Advising on Release of Data (IGARD)

This is an HSCIC (Health and Social Care Information Centre) body with independent members. They look at requests to access health information.

Confidentiality Advisory Group

This group makes sure that access to information is appropriate and in the public interest.

Secure IT Systems

NHS digital provide secure IT systems for use across the NHS. These secure IT systems meet stringent rules and legal regulations. This ensures we follow good practice when looking after sensitive information.

Organisations that wish to use our IT systems must:

  • complete the Information Governance toolkit process
  • sign the Information Governance assurance statement
  • maintain high standards across providers and suppliers
  • fulfil the requirements set out in the Information Governance statement of compliance in order to use the NHS N3 national broadband network

Legal responsibilities

Different types of information come with different legal responsibilities. Many of the rules we follow and abide by can be found in the Health and Social Care Act 2012 and 2014. You can also find more details in the Freedom of Information Act 2000 and the Data Protection Act 1998.
