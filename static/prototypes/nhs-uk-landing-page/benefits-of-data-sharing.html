Benefits of data sharing

Patient health information

Whenever you visit an NHS service, we use and update a record of information all about you. This is known as your Summary Care Record (SCR) and contains your patient health information. This can be seen and used by your GP, your dentist and any hospitals you have visited for treatment.

Inside your record

Your SCR will contain information about the care you have received. This is really important for health and social care professionals. It provides access to your medical history and important details, like allergies and past operations. This information is used to make careful decisions about your care.

Your medical history

Your medical history will include both past and on-going treatments. It will also contain any previous x-rays and discharge notes. The medicine you take, test results and any conditions you have will all be available in your record. This will also include personal data such as your name, age and address.

Sharing your data

Your health records are kept confidential and secure at all times. There are strict laws in place that mean only health and social care professionals can access your medical record. The two most important laws are the Human Rights Act (1998) and the Data Protection Act (1998).

Your data will never be shared for commercial or marketing purposes, unless you personally request for us to do so. The NHS must ensure any personal information is kept secure and only used for reasons to do with your individual care.

However, there is huge potential to use patient health information to improve health and social care services across the NHS. The information can be used to help with research and planning.

Research

Patient data can be used in research to improve treatment for serious illnesses and conditions. This information can also be used to find cures for diseases like diabetes and cancer.

Planning

Patient data can also be used to manage health and social care services. With this information the NHS can plan where to place essential services, like heart and baby care units.

Case study: Breast cancer surgery patients

Sharing data has many positive effects. The length of time spent in hospital for breast cancer surgery patients dropped massively back in 2012. This was all thanks to data from the HSCIC (Health and Social Care Information Centre). The average length of hospital stay was reduced by 50 per cent.
