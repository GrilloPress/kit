When you opt-out

Choosing not to share

You have the right to opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable data with your patient health information. Identifiable data includes your name, address and date of birth. Opting out will mean your identifiable details will no longer appear in data shared by NHS Digital. This will only happen if opt-outs apply to the data being shared.

There are some circumstances where opt-outs do not apply and your personally identifiable data will be used regardless. This includes anytime you visit a health or social care professional for your own direct care purposes.

How opt-out requests are processed

We collect the opt-out information

An automated IT system processes this information

We produce a historic record of information received

We update a record of current opt-outs

The historic record of opt-out information allows NHS Digital to check the date an opt-out was received. It also shows if there has been a withdrawal of an opt-out decision. This demonstrates when and when not to apply a personâ€™s opt-out decision. The record of current opt-outs is used as part of an IT system that applies an opt-out decision to data before it is shared outside of NHS Digital.

The IT system automatically matches data to be shared against NHS numbers held in the record of current opt-outs. When a match is confirmed, all data relating to that NHS number will be removed before the data is released. This includes the NHS number itself.

Time period

Once an opt-out request is received, it can take up to 21 days before it can be applied to future data releases. Once applied, an opt-out will continue to be upheld following a patient's death.

Where this opt-out does not apply

This opt-out will not apply in the following circumstances:

  • where the information is used for purposes relating to your direct care
  • where the personal confidential information held by NHS Digital does not contain an NHS number
  • if you have given consent for your data to be used for a specific reason, like a medical research study
  • if the information is not classed as personal confidential information. This is a combination of data that identifies who you are and information on your health, care or treatment. Personal confidential information is defined in the NHS Act 2006, Section 251, paragraphs 10 and 11
  • where anonymised data means individuals are not identifiable from the information
  • where there is a legal requirement to provide information, such as an order of court
  • where there is an overriding public interest in the disclosure of your data
  • where information is used to support the management of communicable diseases and other risks to public health. This comes under Regulation 3 of the Health Service (Control of Patient Information) Regulations 2002
  • where birth and cancer information is supplied to the Office for National Statistics

National Cancer Registration Service

This opt-out will not apply where information is provided to the National Cancer Registration Service. If you do not want to share your information for this purpose, you can opt-out of this separately.

Assuring transformation data

This opt-out will not apply where the information is provided for the Assuring Transformation collection of data about people with learning disabilities and/or autism, who are in hospital for mental or behavioural healthcare reasons. If you do not want to share your information for this purpose, you can opt-out of this separately.

Assuring transformation data

Previous consent

If you have previously given consent to share your data for another specific reason or research study, and no longer wish for your data to be used in this way, you should contact the organisation you gave consent to.
