Where can I find my NHS number?

You can find your NHS number on any document sent to you by the NHS. This may include:

  • Prescriptions
  • Test results
  • Hospital referral letters
  • Appointment letters
  • Your NHS medical card

Ask your GP for help if you can't find your NHS number

Why might my patient health information be shared?

Your patient health information will only be shared beyond your individual care if it is requested by qualified researchers. Applications to receive this data must be approved by law and the NHS.

Your information will only be released if there is a clear benefit to social care and healthcare services in the UK. This may be used to help plan and manage NHS services. This is really important as it allows the NHS to check the quality of care you receive.

Your information may also be used in research to improve treatment for serious illnesses and diseases such as cancer.

What type of information is shared?

Your gender, date of birth and post code is shared along with your patient health information.

We do not share your name, personal details or anything that can match you personally to your medical data.
