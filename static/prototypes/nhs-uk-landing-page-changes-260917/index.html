Sharing your patient health information

Data Saves Lives

Your data is very important to the NHS. Recording and sharing medical data helps to improve health and social care for you and your family.

Patient health information helps to improve our medical understanding. The NHS depends on this information to provide essential services, like heart units and special baby care wards. We can also use this data to improve treatment for serious illnesses and disease.

By sharing your health data you can contribute to the health of future generations.

Understanding patient health information

Patient health information explained

Your patient health information contains details of your health care and any previous treatments you have received. This is updated any time you visit an NHS service. The data is shared between doctors, hospitals and dentists for reasons linked to your own personal care.

This data can also be shared beyond your personal care to improve health and social care services in the UK. This is done through research and planning. You can choose not to share your personally identifiable data for these reasons.

Why your information might be shared

Personally identifiable data is important for reasons beyond your own personal care. It helps improve health and social care services through research and planning. This includes:

  • helping to improve the health and care services you receive
  • supporting researchers to find cures for serious illnesses

The type of information we share

Your gender, date of birth and post code are all shared along with your patient health information. If you decide to opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable data, we will not share your name or any details that can match you personally to your medical records.

More information on patient data sharing.

You can choose not to share

You have the right to request that your personally identifiable information is not used beyond your own individual care. If you opt-out of sharing your data, this will be respected by all organisations that use health and social care information. This does not include if there is a legal reason or obligation to use your information.
